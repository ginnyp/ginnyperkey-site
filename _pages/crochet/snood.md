## Pattern Summary - with video links!

### Getting Started

1. Make a slip knot  
- [How to make a slip knot](https://www.woolandthegang.com/en/tutorial/how-to-make-a-slip-knot)
2. Chain 32 stitches (or until you reach the desired circumference of your cowl)
- [Chain stitch tutorial](https://www.woolandthegang.com/en/tutorial/how-to-crochet-chain-stitch)
3. Use a slip stitch to join the chain ends into a circle  
- [Crochet a tube](https://www.woolandthegang.com/en/tutorial/how-to-do-tubular-crochet)  
- [Slip stitch tutorial](https://www.woolandthegang.com/en/tutorial/how-to-crochet-slip-stitch)
4. Chain 3 (you won’t turn your work)  
- [Turning chain tutorial](https://www.woolandthegang.com/en/tutorial/how-to-crochet-turning-chain)

### Making Your Snood

5. Double crochet into each chain  
- [Double crochet tutorial](https://www.woolandthegang.com/en/tutorial/how-to-crochet-double-crochet)
6. Continue to double crochet into each new row, working in a spiral
7. Work until you complete 4 rows  
- [Counting rows tutorial](https://www.woolandthegang.com/en/tutorial/how-to-count-rows-in-crochet)

### Finishing

8. In the fifth row:
   - Double crochet into the first 29 stitches
   - Half double crochet into the 30th stitch  
      - [Half double crochet tutorial](https://www.woolandthegang.com/en/tutorial/how-to-work-half-double-crochet)
   - Single crochet into the 31st stitch  
      - [Single crochet tutorial](https://www.woolandthegang.com/en/tutorial/how-to-crochet-single-crochet)
   - Slip stitch into the 32nd stitch  
      - [Slip stitch tutorial](https://www.woolandthegang.com/en/tutorial/how-to-crochet-slip-stitch)
9. Cut the yarn, leave a 5" tail, and pull it through the final loop
10. Weave in your ends  
- [How to weave in ends](https://www.woolandthegang.com/en/tutorial/how-to-weave-in-your-ends-in-crochet)
