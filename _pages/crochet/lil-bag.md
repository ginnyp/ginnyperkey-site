## Lil' Bag Pattern – with video links!

**Get Started**

- Make a **slip knot**, then make **chain stitches** until your chain is about **16 inches long**.
    - [Slip knot tutorial](https://www.woolandthegang.com/en/tutorial/how-to-make-a-slip-knot)
    - [Chain stitch tutorial](https://www.woolandthegang.com/en/tutorial/how-to-crochet-chain-stitch)

- **Join in the round** by inserting your hook into your first chain stitch, yarn over, then pull the yarn through both the chain stitch and the loop on your hook. This will create a circle of chain stitches.
    - [Crochet a tube](https://www.woolandthegang.com/en/tutorial/how-to-do-tubular-crochet)

**Build Your Bag**

- **Single crochet** in every chain stitch.
    - [Single crochet tutorial](https://www.woolandthegang.com/en/tutorial/how-to-crochet-single-crochet)

- After you single crochet in the last chain stitch, **single crochet into the next single crochet stitch**.

- **Continue single crocheting** in spiraled rounds until the bag is 7 inches long. Stop crocheting when you are approximately aligned with the tail from when you joined in the round.

**Add a Handle**

- Lay your bag flat with the tail on either side. **Use stitch markers** to mark where the handle should start and finish on each side of your bag.

- **Count the number of stitches** between each set of stitch markers and write this down.

- Continue crocheting until you reach a stitch marker. **Chain** the number of stitches you counted in the previous step, then **single crochet** in the next stitch marker.

- **Repeat step 8** for the second set of stitch markers.

- Complete **two more rounds** of single crochet, working into the chain stitches on the handle for the first round.

- **Slip stitch** at the end of the last round, then **fasten off** by cutting your yarn and pulling out the final loop.
    - [Slip stitch tutorial](https://www.woolandthegang.com/en/tutorial/how-to-crochet-slip-stitch)

**Seam the Bottom Edge**

- Turn the bag inside out, then use new yarn to single crochet the bottom edges of the bag together.
    - [Single crochet seam tutorial](https://youtu.be/M8SkpizU_0E?si=9f5YnmGZYKmpUdiV&t=257)

- **Fasten off**, then **weave in your ends**.
    - [How to weave in ends](https://www.woolandthegang.com/en/tutorial/how-to-weave-in-your-ends-in-crochet)
